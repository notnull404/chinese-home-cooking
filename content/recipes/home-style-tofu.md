Crispy pan-fried tofu simmered in a savory sauce with peppers and wood ear mushrooms. Heartier than meat.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| tofu | 400g |
| pork | 100g |
| green pepper | 1 pc |
| garlic | 3 cloves |
| green onion | 2 stalks |

## Seasonings

- light soy sauce
- dark soy sauce
- oyster sauce
- Doubanjiang (chili bean paste)
- cornstarch
- salt

## Instructions

1. Cut firm tofu into triangular thick slices. Pat dry with paper towels.

2. Heat oil over medium heat. Pan-fry tofu until golden on both sides. Remove.

3. Leave some oil. Fry Doubanjiang until red oil forms. Add garlic and stir.

4. Add green peppers, red peppers, and wood ear mushrooms. Stir-fry for 1 minute.

5. Return tofu to the wok. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and dark soy sauce. Toss gently.

6. Add a splash of water, cover, and simmer for 1 minute. Thicken with cornstarch slurry. Serve.


## Tips

Use firm tofu — silken tofu won't hold up. Pat the tofu dry before frying to prevent oil splatter. Don't flip too often while pan-frying.
