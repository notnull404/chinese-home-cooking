Numbing and spicy, silky tofu in a rich chili bean sauce. This Sichuan classic will make you eat two bowls of rice.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| tofu | 400g |
| ground pork | 100g |
| doubanjiang | 2 tbsp |
| garlic | 3 cloves |
| ginger | 3 slices |
| green onion | 2 stalks |

## Seasonings

- Doubanjiang (chili bean paste)
- fermented black beans
- ground Sichuan pepper
- chili powder
- light soy sauce
- cornstarch
- salt

## Instructions

1. Cut tofu into 2cm cubes. Blanch in salted boiling water for 2 minutes. Drain gently.

2. Brown the ground pork in hot oil. Remove and set aside.

3. Over low heat, fry Doubanjiang until red oil appears. Add fermented black beans, ginger, garlic, and chili powder.

4. Add a bowl of water, light soy sauce, and salt. Gently slide in the tofu (do not stir).

5. Simmer for 3 minutes over medium heat. Add the cooked pork back.

6. Pour in cornstarch slurry and gently push the tofu to coat (use a pushing motion, not stirring).

7. Plate and sprinkle generously with ground Sichuan pepper and chopped green onion.


## Tips

Blanching the tofu removes the raw bean taste and prevents crumbling. Use a 'pushing' motion, not stirring, to keep the tofu intact. Add Sichuan pepper at the very end for maximum aroma.
