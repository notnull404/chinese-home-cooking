The highest honor for leftover rice. Simple, fast, and delicious when you know the technique.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| rice | 2 cups |
| egg | 2 pcs |
| ham sausage | 1 pc |
| green onion | 2 stalks |

## Seasonings

- salt
- light soy sauce
- white pepper

## Instructions

1. Break up the day-old rice with chopsticks so there are no clumps.

2. Beat eggs with a pinch of salt. Dice ham sausage. Chop green onion.

3. Heat oil in a wok over high heat until smoking.

4. Pour in eggs and quickly scramble with chopsticks into small pieces.

5. Add rice. Press down with the back of a spatula to break up clumps. Stir-fry on high heat.

6. Add ham. Splash light soy sauce along the side of the wok. Sprinkle white pepper.

7. When rice grains start jumping in the wok, add green onion, toss, and serve.


## Tips

Day-old (or slightly dry) rice is essential for fried rice that doesn't clump. Cook on the highest heat possible. Splash soy sauce along the hot wok edge to caramelize it.
