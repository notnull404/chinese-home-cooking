Tender chicken, crunchy peanuts, and a sweet-sour-spicy sauce. The world's most famous Sichuan dish.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| chicken breast | 300g |
| peanuts | 50g |
| dried chili | 10 pcs |
| green onion | 3 stalks |
| garlic | 3 cloves |
| ginger | 3 slices |

## Seasonings

- light soy sauce
- vinegar
- sugar
- cooking wine
- cornstarch
- salt
- dark soy sauce

## Instructions

1. Cut chicken into 1.5cm cubes. Marinate with cooking wine, light soy sauce, and cornstarch for 15 minutes.

2. Mix the sauce: 2 tbsp light soy sauce + 3 tbsp vinegar + 2 tbsp sugar + 1 tbsp cornstarch + 3 tbsp water + a dash of dark soy sauce.

3. Dice cucumber and carrot. Cut dried chilies into segments and remove seeds. Slice ginger and garlic.

4. Slow-fry peanuts in cold oil until golden and crispy. Remove.

5. Stir-fry chicken over high heat until white. Remove.

6. Fry Sichuan peppercorn and dried chilies over low heat until fragrant.

7. Add ginger, garlic, and green onion. Stir-fry carrot and cucumber.

8. Return chicken. Pour in the sauce and stir-fry on high heat. Add peanuts. Serve immediately.


## Tips

Freeze the chicken breast for 15 minutes before cubing for neater cuts. Fry the peanuts in advance and set aside. The sweet-sour ratio is the soul of this dish.
