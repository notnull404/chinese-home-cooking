Silky Cantonese rice porridge with century egg and shredded pork. perfect for breakfast or late-night comfort.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| rice | 1 cup |
| century egg | 2 pcs |
| lean pork | 100g |
| ginger | 3 slices |
| green onion | 2 stalks |

## Seasonings

- salt
- white pepper
- sesame oil
- cooking wine
- cornstarch

## Instructions

1. Soak rice for 30 minutes. Drain and mix with a little oil and salt.

2. Shred pork and marinate with cooking wine, salt, and cornstarch for 10 minutes.

3. Dice century eggs. Julienne ginger. Chop green onion.

4. Bring water to a boil. Add rice and cook over high heat, then reduce to medium-low.

5. Cook for 20 minutes until the rice grains start to burst and the congee becomes silky.

6. Add ginger and century egg. Cook for 5 more minutes.

7. Add pork shreds and stir to separate. Cook for 2 minutes until white.

8. Season with salt and white pepper. Drizzle with sesame oil. Garnish with green onion.


## Tips

Mixing the rice with oil and salt beforehand is the secret to silky congee. Keep the pot at a gentle simmer — don't cover it fully to prevent boiling over.
