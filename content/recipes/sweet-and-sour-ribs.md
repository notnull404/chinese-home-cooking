Crispy on the outside, tender inside, coated in a glossy sweet-sour glaze. A favorite for kids and adults alike.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| pork ribs | 500g |
| tomato sauce | 3 tbsp |
| vinegar | 2 tbsp |
| sugar | 2 tbsp |
| ginger | 3 slices |
| sesame seeds | 1 tbsp |

## Seasonings

- cooking wine
- light soy sauce
- vinegar
- sugar
- ketchup
- cornstarch
- salt

## Instructions

1. Cut ribs into small pieces. Soak in cold water for 30 minutes to remove blood.

2. Blanch ribs in cold water with ginger and cooking wine. Skim foam and drain.

3. Mix Sweet-Sour Sauce: 3 tbsp sugar + 3 tbsp vinegar + 2 tbsp light soy sauce + 1 tbsp ketchup + 1 tbsp cornstarch + half bowl water.

4. Heat oil to 70% hot. Fry ribs over medium heat for 4–5 minutes until golden.

5. Turn heat to high and fry for another 30 seconds to make extra crispy. Remove.

6. Leave a little oil. Sauté green onion, ginger, and garlic. Pour in the sauce and simmer until thickened.

7. Toss ribs in the sauce until well coated. Sprinkle sesame seeds. Serve immediately for maximum crunch.


## Tips

The double-fry step (first to cook through, second at higher heat) is the secret to extra crispy ribs. Mix the sauce in advance. Serve immediately while still crunchy.
