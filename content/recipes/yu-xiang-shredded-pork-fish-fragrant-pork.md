A classic Sichuan dish — tender shredded pork in a sweet-sour-spicy sauce. No fish involved, but unforgettable flavor.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| pork tenderloin | 250g |
| wood ear mushroom | 50g |
| carrot | 1 pc |
| green pepper | 1 pc |
| garlic | 3 cloves |
| ginger | 3 slices |
| green onion | 2 stalks |

## Seasonings

- light soy sauce
- vinegar
- sugar
- cooking wine
- cornstarch
- Doubanjiang (chili bean paste)
- salt

## Instructions

1. Slice pork into thin shreds. Marinate with cooking wine, light soy sauce, and cornstarch for 15 minutes.

2. Mix the 'Fish-Fragrant Sauce': 2 tbsp light soy sauce + 2 tbsp vinegar + 1 tbsp sugar + 1 tbsp cornstarch + 3 tbsp water.

3. Julienne carrot, cucumber, and wood ear mushrooms. Mince garlic and ginger.

4. Heat oil in wok over high heat. Stir-fry pork until white. Remove and set aside.

5. Leave some oil. Add Doubanjiang (chili bean paste) and stir until red oil releases. Add garlic and ginger.

6. Add carrot and wood ear mushrooms, stir-fry for 2 minutes. Add cucumber.

7. Return pork to the wok. Pour in the sauce. Stir-fry on high heat until sauce thickens. Sprinkle green onion and serve.


## Tips

Freeze the pork for 30 minutes before slicing for cleaner cuts. Fry the Doubanjiang until you see red oil — this is key for color and flavor. Mix the sauce beforehand for a quick stir-fry.
