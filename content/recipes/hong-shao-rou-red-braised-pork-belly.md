Rich, caramelized pork belly — melt-in-your-mouth comfort food that pairs perfectly with steamed rice.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| pork belly | 500g |
| green onion | 3 stalks |
| ginger | 5 slices |
| rock sugar | 30g |
| light soy sauce | 2 tbsp |
| dark soy sauce | 1 tbsp |
| cooking wine | 2 tbsp |

## Seasonings

- rock sugar
- light soy sauce
- dark soy sauce
- cooking wine
- salt

## Instructions

1. Cut pork belly into 3cm cubes. Blanch in cold water with ginger and cooking wine. Skim foam and drain.

2. In a dry pan over low heat, fry pork belly pieces until golden on all sides and some fat renders out. Remove.

3. Leave a little oil in the pan. Add rock sugar and cook on low heat until it melts into a deep amber color with bubbles.

4. Immediately add pork back and stir to coat with caramel. Add green onion, ginger, star anise, cinnamon, and bay leaf.

5. Add 2 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 tbsp dark soy sauce, 2 tbsp cooking wine. Stir well.

6. Pour in enough boiling water to just cover the meat. Bring to a boil then reduce to low heat.

7. Cover and simmer for 40–50 minutes. Flip the meat halfway through.

8. Remove the lid and turn up the heat to reduce the sauce until thick and glossy, coating each piece. Serve.


## Tips

Always use boiling water to braise the meat (cold water toughens it). Cook the caramelized sugar slowly over low heat. Stay near the pan during the final sauce reduction — it can burn quickly.
